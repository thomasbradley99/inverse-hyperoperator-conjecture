\section{Conjecture}

Let $f$ be a holomorphic operator whose inverse requires $r$ independent
branch parameters $(b_1,\dots,b_r)\in\mathbb{Z}^r$ under analytic
continuation. We call $r$ the \emph{branch rank}.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Conjecture.}\;
Any globally continuous single-valued representation of the full inverse
of $f$ requires at least $r$ extra real coordinates beyond the base value
coordinates. Over $\mathbb{C}$ this gives
$\dim_{\mathbb{R}} \geq 2 + r$.

\section{First Prediction: Inverse of $z^z$}

The map $w = z^z = e^{z\log z}$ has two independent sources of
multivaluedness in its inverse: a logarithm branch $k\in\mathbb{Z}$
and a Lambert-$W$ branch $m\in\mathbb{Z}$. Hence $r=2$ and the
conjecture predicts $\dim_{\mathbb{R}} \geq 4$.

The only 4-real-dimensional normed division algebra is~$\mathbb{H}$.

\section{The Dimensional Ladder}

The conjecture applies where inverses produce genuine monodromy
(continuous multivaluedness):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Inverse of & Branch rank $r$ & Predicted $\dim_{\mathbb{R}}$ & Algebra \\
\midrule
$e^x$ (logarithm) & 1 & $1{+}1=2$ & $\mathbb{C}$ \\
$z^z$              & 2 & $2{+}2=4$ & $\mathbb{H}\;?$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The earlier extensions $\mathbb{N}\!\to\!\mathbb{Z}$ (subtraction) and
$\mathbb{Z}\!\to\!\mathbb{Q}$ (division) are algebraic closures under
discrete inverse operations, not monodromy phenomena. The conjecture's
scope begins where analytic continuation is meaningful.

\subsection*{Why normed division algebras?}

By the Hurwitz theorem, the only finite-dimensional normed division
algebras over $\mathbb{R}$ are $\mathbb{R},\mathbb{C},\mathbb{H},
\mathbb{O}$ (dimensions $1,2,4,8$). There are many 4-dimensional real
algebras, but only $\mathbb{H}$ is a normed division algebra. The
stronger form of the conjecture predicts the extension lands on the
next Hurwitz algebra specifically --- not just any $\mathbb{R}^4$.

\section{Two Forms of the Conjecture}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{General:} branch rank $r$ forces $r$ extra real coordinates.
\item \textbf{Structural (stronger):} the extensions reproduce the normed
      division algebra sequence
      $\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{C}\to\mathbb{H}$, with the algebra
      structure forced by monodromy.
\end{itemize}

Computational evidence for the $r=2$ prediction is in the
\texttt{computational-gap-detection/} folder. No formal proof is claimed.
